Welcome to my Life

By Maria Melissa Caballero

I wanted to create this page so you can know a little bit about me and what I know so far about programming and coding.

First of all, I was born in Mexico City. I pretty much completed most of my schooling in Mexico but when I was a Junior in Highschool I moved to the United States. It was pretty hard at first to adjust to this new life, but thanfully I had my parents who moved here with me too, so they helped me a lot to overcome my sadness at that time.

I am not gonna lie. I wanted to move back, because I left everything. But most importantly, I left my family and friends. When I was here there was times where I couldn't stop thinking about them. I was missing them like crazy. However, I knew I was here already and I had to fight to fulfill my dreams and goals. I didn't want to give up.

Later on, I got used to this new life I had. I met new people. I had friends that were international students. I also met someone special for me at that time. He is still special though, but in another way. He and I build memories together. However, we are continuing separate lifes now, but I'm glad we are still friends.

Furthermore, I continued College right after Highschool. At first, I was studying to be a nurse, but then I changed my mind because I realized that everybody wanted to be a nurse, so I changed to Biochemistry, and lastly, with all this situation of the coronavirus going on, the biochemistry major didn't sound as appealing to me anymore. I came to see that everybody in this world is moving through with the aid of the internet. Everybody uses either an app or a website, or any other thing to be able to communicate to others, to learn, to raise their businesses, etc. Therefore, now I am a new upcoming Software Developer with an already completed Associates in Science.

Technology is in constant change. It has been at peek now a days and that is why I want to be able to be good at it. I am learning JavaScript, Java, Python, SQL server. However, I am mainly focusing on full stack JavaScript.
